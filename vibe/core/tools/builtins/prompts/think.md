Use the think tool to reason through a problem before acting.

## When to use think

- Before multi-step tasks: plan your approach before making changes
- After reading complex code: analyze what you've seen before deciding what to do
- When facing a decision with multiple options: evaluate trade-offs before committing
- After a tool call returns unexpected results: reason about what went wrong

## When NOT to use think

- For simple, single-step tasks (just do them)
- When you already know exactly what to do
- Repeatedly without taking action (think once, then act)

## Tips

- Be specific in your reasoning, not vague
- Structure your thoughts: what do I know, what do I need, what's my plan
- One think call is usually enough - don't overthink
